The Power of Silence in Storytelling

While writing Aava, I noticed what I kept removing. Every sentence became a negotiation: what is spoken, what stays between the lines, and what silence is allowed to carry.

Silence as active tension

In psychological suspense, silence is not a pause. It is a force. The moment language pulls back, readers begin to fill the space with their own fears, memories, and expectations.

That is where tension sharpens. Not in explanation, but in the pressure of what almost appears.

How I write silence

Rhythm matters first: shorter lines, deliberate gaps, fewer explanations. I rely on sensory detail instead of exposition, so the body notices before the mind can categorize.

"Silence is not the absence of meaning. It is meaning without permission."

In Aava, silence behaves like a character: it follows, observes, and slowly changes shape scene by scene.
